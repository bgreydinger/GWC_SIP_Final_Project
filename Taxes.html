Taxes


Before diving in headfirst into filing taxes, it's important to familiarize yourself with the vocabulary:

EARNED INCOME- salaries, wages, tips, taxable scholarships or grants

UNEARNED INCOME- investments, interest on savings, unemployment compensation, etc.

GROSS INCOME- total income in the form of money, goods, property, etc. that is not tax-exempt

EXEMPTIONS- a preset amount of money that can be deducted from the taxable amount for basic living expenses. You and any dependents qualify.

STANDARD DEDUCTION- money that the government gives you if you qualify. It differs through different marital statuses

ITEMIZED DEDUCTIONS- there are 6 main categories of deductables:

Medical/dental, Taxes, Interest, Charity contributions, Casualty loss/theft

These deductions lower the taxes owed.

W-2 FORMS- these are wage-income forms from employers. They help prepare for filing taxes.

FILING STATUS- single/married/head of household status, with other sub-categories

Depending on your income, you may or may not be required to file taxes. In Washington State 2019, if you made less than $12,000 a year, you would most likely not need to file your taxes.

Your state also determines what you are taxed on. Washington State has a high sales tax, but no income tax. On the other hand, Oregon has no sales tax but does have an income tax.

There are different forms to file:

1040EZ- shortest/simplest

1040A-longer, has more flexibility

1040- "long form" for income over $50,000
